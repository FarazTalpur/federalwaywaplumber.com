Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include persistent leaks, rust-colored water, low water pressure, frequent clogs, and visible corrosion or damage.

The lifespan of pipes varies: copper pipes last 50-70 years, PVC pipes 25-40 years, and galvanized steel 20-50 years. Regular inspections can help determine their condition.

First, turn off the water supply to prevent further damage. Then, contact a professional plumber immediately to assess and repair the issue.

Costs can vary widely based on the extent of damage, materials used, and labor rates. Pipe repair can range from $150 to $550, while replacements can cost $2,000 to $15,000.

Minor leaks might be fixable with DIY methods, but it is safer and more effective to hire a professional to ensure proper repair and minimize risk of further damage.
