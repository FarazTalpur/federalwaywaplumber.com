Frequently Asked Questions

Water pressure testing is a procedure used to measure the pressure of water in a home or commercial plumbing system to ensure it is within safe and optimal levels.

Testing water pressure is important to prevent damage to plumbing fixtures and appliances, ensure efficient water usage, and protect against leaks and bursts caused by excessively high pressure.

Water pressure testing is typically done using a pressure gauge, which is attached to a water outlet like a hose bib or faucet. The pressure is then read from the gauge and compared to recommended pressure levels.

Normal residential water pressure usually ranges between 40 and 80 psi (pounds per square inch). Levels above 80 psi can cause damage over time, while pressure below 40 psi may lead to inadequate water flow.

If water pressure is outside the recommended range, it may be necessary to install a pressure regulator if the pressure is too high, or contact a plumber to diagnose and resolve low pressure issues, which could indicate a leak or blockage.
