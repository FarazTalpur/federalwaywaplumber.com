Frequently Asked Questions

The main elements include the water supply system, the drainage system, and the fixtures such as sinks, faucets, showers, and toilets. These components must be properly installed and tested to ensure efficient operation and compliance with local building codes.

A plumbing rough-in refers to the phase where all the water supply and drainage pipes are installed within the walls and floors, without the fixtures attached. This stage is crucial as it allows for inspections and modifications before the walls are closed up.

Common materials include PEX (cross-linked polyethylene), CPVC (chlorinated polyvinyl chloride), copper, and PVC (polyvinyl chloride) for pipework. The choice of material often depends on local building codes, budget, and specific project needs.

The average cost of plumbing for new construction can vary widely based on the size and complexity of the project, but it generally ranges from $1.50 to $5 per square foot. Costs can increase with the selection of high-end materials and fixtures.

It's essential to hire a licensed and experienced plumbing contractor familiar with local building codes and regulations. They will design and install the plumbing system to comply with all necessary standards and will coordinate required inspections during construction.
